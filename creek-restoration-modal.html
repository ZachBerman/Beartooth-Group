Creek Restoration

According to the U.S. EPA over 55% of U.S. waterways are in poor health. They provide insufficient habitat for wildlife, fail to adequately cleanse water, and magnify the impacts of floods due to human alterations. We restore creeks and streams to improve and provide greater habitat for fish and other wildlife species. Our restoration efforts result in lower water temperature, cleaner water, slower moving water (minimizing the impacts of floods), and recharge the aquifer. We have restored roughly 50 miles of creeks and rivers to date.

Pond & Wetland Restoration

According to the U.S. EPA over 50% of U.S. wetlands have been destroyed and an additional nine square miles are lost each day. We restore wetlands and the associated creeks, creating habitat for wildlife and allowing wetlands to perform their critical ecosystem services: recharging aquifers, cleaning water, reducing flooding and more. We have restored roughly 3 square miles of wetlands to date.

Structure Removal & Remediation

According to the U.S. Office of Ecosystem Services, the U.S. is losing nearly ten square miles of land to development each day. Roughly two of these ten square miles are farms and ranches. This conversion brings people, roads, traffic, fences and energy consumption. We remove damaged, dangerous and uninhabitable structures and other wildlife un-friendly obstacles. We remove and/or remediate the structures, remove fencing and use wildlife-friendly fencing. We use conservation easements and other tools to permanently protect land. Through this work, including the protection of over twenty square miles of ranch land, we have seen the return of elk and other wildlife to areas they had not been able to reach for decades.

Mine Reclamation & Other

Improperly cleaned up chemical use and mineral extraction can leave a blight on the landscape. We clean up the remains of hazardous chemical use and inappropriately remediated mining extraction. To date, this has included the remains of gold and coal mining operations, methamphetamine laboratories and hazardous chemicals that have leached into the soil.
